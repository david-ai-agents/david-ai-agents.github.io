# Here is the strategic breakdown for your "AI Readiness Audit" Spreadsheet.

This spreadsheet is designed to do the selling for you. By the time a prospect fills this out, the math should vividly demonstrate that not hiring you is costing them more than your fee.

## We will divide the audit into three sections: 
* The Cost of Inefficiency, Technical Complexity, and Strategic Value.

üìä Section 1: The "Bleeding Neck" (ROI Calculator)

Goal: Prove they are losing at least $50k/year on this problem so a $15k-$30k proposal looks cheap.
### Q1. Task Frequency & Volume
"How many times per month is this specific process performed across the entire company?"
Why this matters: Automation thrives on volume. Low volume = low ROI. High volume = high ROI.

### Q2. Human Time per Instance
"On average, how many minutes does it take a human to complete one instance of this task (including switching tabs, logging in, thinking time)?"
Why this matters: Executives usually underestimate this. 5 minutes x 1000 times a month = 83 hours lost.

### Q3. Fully Burdened Hourly Cost
"What is the average hourly cost of the employee doing this task? (Salary + Overhead usually $\approx$ $40-$80/hr)."
Why this matters: This is the anchor.

**Formula to show them: (Q1 * Q2 / 60) * Q3 = Monthly Waste.**

### Q4. The "Cost of Error"
"What is the estimated financial impact of a single human error in this process (e.g., lost lead, refund, compliance fine, reputation damage)?"
Why this matters: This is the "fear" factor. If one missed email costs a $5k deal, your automation pays for itself instantly.

Why this matters: Unstructured data is the hardest problem to solve and the highest value service you offer.
